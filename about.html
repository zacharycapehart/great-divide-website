Our Story

About The Great Divide

The Great Divide sits at the crossroads of two Indianapolis neighborhoods — Lockerbie Square to the north and Holy Cross to the south. The name reflects where we are, and what we do: bring people together across that divide.

We are a neighborhood pub in the truest sense. Regulars know each other by name. First-time visitors quickly feel at home. The staff knows what you drink before you order it. It is the kind of place that becomes part of your routine.

Our food is pub food — familiar, satisfying, and done well. Our drinks are cold, plentiful, and served without pretense. Most evenings there is live music filling the room, whether that is a piano player or a local band.

If you live near Lockerbie Square or Holy Cross, this is your pub. If you are visiting Indianapolis and want to experience a real neighborhood place instead of a tourist spot, you found the right door.

The Neighborhood

Located between Lockerbie Square and Holy Cross, two of Indianapolis’s oldest and most distinctive neighborhoods.

The Atmosphere

Warm, unpretentious, and social. The kind of place where conversations start easily and last longer than expected.

The Regulars

We have plenty of them. And they will probably say hello when you come in.
