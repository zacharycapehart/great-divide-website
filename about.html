Our Story

About The Great Divide

The Great Divide is on East New York Street in Indianapolis, between the Lockerbie Square and Holy Cross neighborhoods. Lockerbie Square is to the west, Holy Cross is to the east, and we sit right in the middle. We have been a part of this stretch of the city for years, and we are not going anywhere.

It is a neighborhood pub. The regulars know each other, and the staff knows the regulars. If you have been in a few times, there is a good chance your bartender already knows what you drink. New faces are welcome here the same as familiar ones.

The food is pub food — burgers, fish and chips, shepherd’s pie, soup of the day. Nothing on the menu is trying to be something it is not. It is the kind of food people come back for, not because it is fancy, but because it is good and it is consistent.

The bar is cold beer, good whiskey, and wine by the glass. The drinks are straightforward and well-poured. Pull up a stool, stay a while. Nobody will rush you out.

Most evenings there is live music. Usually a piano player or a local musician. It stays at a volume where you can still have a conversation, which is how we prefer it.

If you live nearby, this is your local. If you are visiting Indianapolis and want to sit somewhere that feels like the real city rather than a tourist stop, come find us on New York Street. Order a drink. You will see what we mean.

The Neighborhood

Lockerbie Square to the west, Holy Cross to the east. The Great Divide sits between them — and has for years.

The Atmosphere

Warm, unpretentious, and social. The room is comfortable in the way that only comes with time. Nothing here was designed to look lived-in. It simply is.

The Regulars

We have plenty of them. They know each other by name, and they will probably say hello when you come in.
