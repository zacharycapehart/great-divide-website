# Great Divide Brand Agent

## Purpose
Ensure that all content, design, and messaging on the Great Divide Restaurant website aligns with the restaurant's brand identity and voice.

---

## Brand Identity

### Name
**Great Divide Restaurant**

### Tagline
*Farm to Table — Seasonal ingredients, bold flavors, neighborhood atmosphere.*

### Brand Values
1. **Freshness** – Locally sourced, seasonal, and sustainable ingredients.
2. **Community** – A welcoming neighborhood gathering place.
3. **Authenticity** – Honest food, honest service.
4. **Quality** – Attention to detail in every dish and guest interaction.

### Brand Voice & Tone
| Context | Voice |
|---------|-------|
| Headlines | Warm, confident, inviting |
| Body copy | Conversational, genuine, approachable |
| CTAs | Direct and action-oriented |
| Social media | Friendly, enthusiastic, community-focused |

---

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette
| Token | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| `--accent` | `#8b3f2f` | Primary brand color, CTAs, highlights |
| `--accent-dark` | `#6f3124` | Hover states, emphasis |
| `--bg` | `#f9f6f1` | Page background |
| `--surface` | `#ffffff` | Cards, header, footer |
| `--surface-muted` | `#f0ebe3` | Section backgrounds, subtle separation |
| `--text` | `#1f1f1f` | Body text |
| `--border` | `#ddd4c7` | Borders, dividers |

### Typography
- **Headings:** Serif or clean sans-serif, conveying warmth and elegance.
- **Body:** `Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif` — readable and accessible.
- **Eyebrow labels:** Uppercase, wide letter-spacing, small size, accent color.

### Logo
- Text-based logo: **Great Divide**
- Should appear prominent in the header on all pages.

---

## Brand Audit Checklist
- [ ] Logo appears clearly in the site header.
- [ ] Color palette matches brand tokens exactly.
- [ ] All copy reflects the defined brand voice.
- [ ] No off-brand fonts or colors introduced.
- [ ] CTAs use the approved accent color.
- [ ] Tagline or brand statement visible on the homepage hero.
- [ ] Image choices (if any) reflect freshness, warmth, and community.

---

## Out-of-Scope / Anti-Patterns
- Avoid corporate or cold language.
- Avoid overly casual slang that undermines quality perception.
- Never use colors outside the defined palette without brand approval.
- Do not use generic stock-photo imagery that feels disconnected from the brand.
